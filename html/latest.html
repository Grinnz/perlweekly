Articles

Relationships with optional foreign key values (DBIx::Class)

David Schmidt (davewood) explained the problem: "Imagine two related tables 'human' and 'cat': A cat belongs to a human but when the human dies the cat can continue to roam freely." I am not a cat-person, but based on all the information I gather on the web, and there is no shortage of information about cats, I wonder if the word 'belongs' can even apply there. Anyway, the problem with the database still remains.

Perl - understand List, unary operation and array in scalar context

Probably in response to the article of Dave Cross from last week (though unfortunately he does not link there), Celogeek gives a few examples for list and scalar context.

Testing

Two testing heavy weights in one issue! Nice.

Merry Christmas! Parallel testing with Test::Class::Moose has arrived

If you were looking for a Christmas present, here is one from Ovid. He says he can make your test 24 times faster. Or at least, the one he had was mad so much faster. Oh and he is using Parallel::ForkManager. Lucky I just fixed a stupid bug there that I introduced a while ago.

Profile Your Tests (One Test Class Per File)

chromatic agrees with 90% of what Ovid write in another article but does not tell us the 10% he disagrees with. But then he goes on explaining why he thinks using Test::Class was probably a good choice in their case. If you write test, and I really hope you do, then it is worth reading these articles and thinking about the implications.

Code

Graphics::Potrace

Potrace is a tool to transform bitmaps into vector graphics. Graphics::Potrace, written by Flavio Poletti provides the Perl binding to it.

Pjam - continues integration for Perl, using Pinto.

Keyword: DevOps.

Fun

Christmas Eve Obfu

If it's Christmas, you need a tree. A tree made of Perl. See what Toby Inkster had in mind.

UAV::Pilot v0.8 Released - Now Supports Wumpus Rover

The Wumpus Rover was build on top of an old RC car using a Raspberry Pi and an Arduino, and controlled by Timm Murray via his UAV::Pilot module. The article comes with a video, that was also featured on the Perl TV. If you are looking to build something by yourself or with you kids, this can be a great project.

Perl and Me, Part 4: A Worthy Program, Exceedingly Well Read

Buddy Burden continues telling his story. This time he explains why readability of code is so important, and how that does not mean limited language.

Web

Perl Jedi, Hello World !

Jedi is a web application framework with no DSL. Celogeek, who put on his Joda-face when creating the Jedi, has started a series of articles. This is the first part and he already published a follow-up on plugin session. I certainly like the fact that he did not just put it on CPAN and waited for the hordes of people to start using it. In these days one has to explain even a Jedi.
